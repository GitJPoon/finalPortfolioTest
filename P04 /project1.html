Dine Out Vancouver App Design

Year: 2014

Role: Ideator, Prototyper

Type: Group Academic

Team Members: Me, Solomon Hsu, Bianna Zhu, Tommy Ma

A smartphone or tablet application that will assist users in finding and organizing all necessary information during the vancouver dineout event.

Design Issue

The idea of Dineout can be confusing to some people that have never heard of it or are new to the event. Addtionally, since there is currently no application that exist in assisting users with the Dine Out event, our application concept aims to achieve at solving this problem. The purpose of this application will help users e ectively gather various restaurant information, as well as help organize and make reservations at a much smoother and efficient pace.

WireFrames

We then moved into the process of wireframing. This allows us to get a general idea of how the application will work and how the application will flow for users. Wireframing allows us to present our idea for feedback as well as develop solutions for problems. Additionally, Wireframing allows us to showcase our application’s architecture visually, allows for clarification and helps make the design process iterative.

Prototyping

After wireframing, we transition to creating a mockup. Creating a mockup allows us to experiment with the design. This allows us to see if our proposed color scheme will fit with the overall feel of the application. In addition, when creating a mockup we can check if our pictures fit with the usability of the application. By creating a mockup, we can quickly test different features of our application to see if it works for the general audience.
